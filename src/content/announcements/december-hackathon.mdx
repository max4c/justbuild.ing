# December Hackathon: Build for Good

We're excited to announce our winter hackathon taking place December 8-9, 2023, at Kiln Workspace in Lehi. This hackathon will focus on developing projects with positive social impact.

## Theme: Build for Good

For this hackathon, we're challenging participants to create solutions that address social, environmental, or community challenges. This could include:

- Tools for nonprofit organizations
- Environmental monitoring or conservation solutions
- Education technology for underserved communities
- Health and wellness applications
- Accessibility improvements for existing technologies

The theme is meant to inspire, but teams are free to work on any project they believe creates positive impact.

## Schedule

### Friday, December 8
- **6:00-7:00 PM**: Check-in, Introductions & Team Formation
- **7:00-8:00 PM**: Keynote: "Tech for Impact" by Sarah Johnson, CTO of GreenTech Solutions
- **8:00 PM onwards**: Building begins

### Saturday, December 9
- **All morning**: Building continues
- **12:00-1:00 PM**: Lunch & Lightning Talk on "Designing for Inclusivity"
- **1:00-2:30 PM**: Final presentations
- **2:30-3:00 PM**: Judging and awards
- **3:00-4:00 PM**: Networking and wrap-up

## Prizes

This hackathon will feature prizes for different categories:

- **Most Impactful Solution**: $1,000
- **Best Technical Implementation**: $750
- **Most Innovative Concept**: $500
- **People's Choice Award**: $250

Additionally, our sponsors will be offering mentorship opportunities and cloud credits for standout projects.

## Judging Criteria

Projects will be judged based on:

1. **Impact potential**: How effectively could this solution address the chosen problem?
2. **Innovation**: How original and creative is the approach?
3. **Technical implementation**: How well was the solution executed?
4. **Presentation**: How clearly was the project and its impact communicated?

## Registration

Registration is free but limited to 40 participants. Please register by December 1 to secure your spot.

Food and beverages will be provided throughout the event. Participants are encouraged to bring their own laptops and any hardware they might need for their projects.

## COVID Safety

We will follow current local health guidelines. Hand sanitizer will be available, and we encourage participants to stay home if they're feeling unwell.

## Code of Conduct

All participants are expected to adhere to our community code of conduct. We're committed to providing a harassment-free, inclusive experience for everyone.
