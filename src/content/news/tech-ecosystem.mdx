# The Growing Tech Ecosystem in Utah County

While the Salt Lake area often gets the spotlight as part of "Silicon Slopes," Utah County has been quietly building its own vibrant tech ecosystem. From Provo to Lehi, innovative companies and talented builders are creating a distinct tech community with its own unique strengths and opportunities.

## Beyond the Big Names

Utah County is home to tech giants like Adobe, Qualtrics, and Domo, but the real story is in the growing number of startups and scale-ups choosing to build here. Companies like Podium, Divvy (now part of Bill.com), and MX have demonstrated that Utah County can produce billion-dollar tech businesses.

According to recent data from the Utah Valley Entrepreneurship Center, over 150 tech startups were founded in Utah County in the past three years alone, with total venture funding exceeding $900 million.

## Why Utah County?

Several factors contribute to the area's appeal for tech builders:

1. **University talent pipeline**: BYU and UVU produce thousands of STEM graduates each year, creating a steady flow of technical talent.

2. **Quality of life**: Lower cost of living compared to traditional tech hubs, combined with outdoor recreation opportunities and family-friendly communities.

3. **Supportive business environment**: Utah consistently ranks as one of the best states for business, with favorable regulations and tax incentives.

4. **Community mindset**: A culture that values collaboration over competition has fostered numerous formal and informal networks for founders and developers.

## The Missing Pieces

Despite its strengths, Utah County's tech ecosystem still has room for growth. In conversations with local founders and developers, several common challenges emerge:

- **Early-stage funding**: While later-stage capital is increasingly available, seed funding can still be harder to access than in more established tech hubs.

- **Technical networking**: Developers often report difficulty finding high-quality technical communities focused on cutting-edge technologies and practices.

- **Diversity and inclusion**: The tech sector here, like many places, continues to work toward better representation and inclusion.

## How justbuild.ing Fits In

Our community aims to address one specific gap in the ecosystem: creating a space where top builders can connect, collaborate, and elevate each other's work. Through monthly hackathons and ongoing projects, we're fostering deeper technical relationships that can lead to company formation and innovation.

"What makes Silicon Valley special isn't just the capital or the big companies—it's the density of technical relationships," explains Jacob Wright, justbuild.ing co-founder. "We're trying to create that same density here, where the best builders know each other and can quickly form effective teams."

## Looking Ahead

As we move into 2024, Utah County's tech ecosystem shows no signs of slowing down. Several trends to watch include:

- Growing specialization in enterprise SaaS, fintech, and healthtech
- Emerging AI startups leveraging local data science talent
- Increased interest from out-of-state investors and accelerators
- More "boomerang" talent returning from coastal tech hubs

For builders looking to be part of this growing ecosystem, the opportunities have never been better. Whether you're looking to join an existing company or start something new, the community and resources to support your journey are increasingly available right here in Utah County.
